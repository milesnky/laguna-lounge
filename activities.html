Activities & Experiences

Home

1. Private, Safe, and Inclusive Environment

Access is strictly members-only, with rigorous identity verification — no tourists or random walk-ins. Our house rules enforce zero tolerance for aggression, exploitation, or irresponsible behaviour. The space is discreet, respectful, and mature — more like a private cultural lounge than a public venue.

2. Cultural and Creative Programming

As an asociación cultural, our activities are designed to enrich members creatively, socially, and intellectually.

  • Music & Art: live DJ sessions, open mics, karaoke nights, painting jams, or even music production workshops.
  • Studio Corners: some clubs have small music studios for members to collaborate or produce tracks together.
  • Exhibitions: local artists showcase their work in the club’s lounge.
  • Movie Nights: projections of documentaries or films relevant to culture, cannabis, or wellness.
  • Gaming & Recreation: chess, board games, chess, or trivia.

3. Education and Harm-Reduction Focus

We are responsible CSC that often includes educational activities to promote safe consumption and knowledge. Examples:

  • Lectures or talks on harm reduction, cannabis science, or law.
  • Workshops on cultivation, extraction, or sustainability.
  • Guest speakers — doctors, lawyers, or activists explaining rights and health.
  • Public awareness campaigns (internally) about responsible use, mental health, or substance mixing.

4. Community Events and Member Interaction

CSCs foster genuine connection. Members might gather for:

  • Game nights, board games, chess, or trivia.
  • Food events, like tapas evenings, shared meals, or BBQs.
  • Wellness activities — yoga, meditation, or art therapy.
  • Volunteer or charity events, like collecting food or supporting local causes.

5. A "Third Place" for Members

Sociologically, many members describe our CSC as a “third place” — not home, not work, but a safe and friendly environment where they can relax, be themselves, and connect with others. It’s calm, private, and community-driven, compared to nightlife venues or bars. Staff are trained to maintain a peaceful, respectful vibe. Members take part in maintaining the space, decorating, or organizing activities.

6. Purpose Beyond Cannabis

Ultimately, we position ourselves as social, cultural, and educational space where cannabis is one part of a broader, responsible community experience. We help reduce stigma by showing cannabis users can be healthy, creative, and responsible. We build micro-communities of artists, professionals, and locals. Promote alternative culture, sustainability, and mental well-being.
